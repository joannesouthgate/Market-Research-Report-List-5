<p><p>The Hepatitis A vaccine market comprises primarily two types: inactivated vaccines and live attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines utilize killed virus particles, providing immunity without risk of disease, making them safe for all populations, including immunocompromised individuals. In contrast, live attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of the virus, which can stimulate a stronger immune response but may not be suitable for certain groups. Both vaccine types aim to prevent Hepatitis A infection, catering to different demographics and health needs.</p></p>
