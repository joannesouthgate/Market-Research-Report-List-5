<p><p>The electrostatic precipitator (ESP) market is primarily divided into two types: wet and dry. Wet electrostatic precipitators utilize a liquid medium to enhance particle capture efficiency, making them suitable for industries with high humidity or corrosive emissions. On the other hand, dry electrostatic precipitators operate without water, relying on electrical fields to attract and collect particulate matter. This makes them efficient for dust-heavy processes, particularly in the power generation and cement sectors, where energy savings and maintenance are critical.</p></p>
