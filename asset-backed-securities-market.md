<p><ul><li>Existing Assets</li><li>Future Cash Flow</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Asset-backed securities (ABS) can be categorized into two primary market types: existing assets and future cash flow markets. Existing assets refer to financial instruments backed by tangible assets, such as mortgages or auto loans, with cash flows derived from these assets' existing performance. Conversely, the future cash flow market involves securities backed by projected earnings from assets yet to be developed or realized, such as royalties or future leases. Both markets provide liquidity and investment opportunities based on different risk profiles.</p></p>
