<p><p>The melanoma diagnostics and therapeutics market is categorized into various types based on melanoma characteristics. Nodular melanoma is aggressive and grows rapidly, while superficial spreading melanoma is the most common variant, typically appearing as a flat, irregular patch. Amelanotic melanoma lacks pigmentation, making it harder to diagnose. Other types include lentigo maligna and acral lentiginous melanoma. These categories influence diagnostic approaches and treatment modalities, which range from surgical excision to immunotherapy and targeted therapies, tailored to specific melanoma types.</p></p>
