<p><p>Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL) is an engineered wood product made from multiple layers of thin wood veneers bonded together with adhesives, enhancing strength and stability. The market for LVL is categorized into two main types: structural and non-structural. Structural LVL is used in beams, headers, and joists, providing support in construction applications. In contrast, non-structural LVL is used for interior applications like furniture or decorative elements, where strength is secondary to aesthetic appeal. Both types offer versatility and sustainability.</p></p>
