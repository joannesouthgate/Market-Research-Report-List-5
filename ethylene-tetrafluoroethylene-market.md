<p><p>The Ethylene Tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE) market comprises two main types: ETFE injection molding and ETFE extrusion molding. ETFE injection molding involves injecting molten ETFE into molds to create complex shapes and components, offering high precision and durability. In contrast, ETFE extrusion molding involves forcing ETFE through a die to produce sheets, tubes, or profiles, enabling large-scale production with consistent properties. Both methods leverage ETFE's excellent chemical resistance, lightweight nature, and thermal stability, making them suitable for various industrial applications.</p></p>
