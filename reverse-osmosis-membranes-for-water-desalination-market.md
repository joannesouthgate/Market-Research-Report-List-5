<p><ul><li>Composite Membranes</li><li>Asymmetric Membranes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Reverse osmosis membranes for water desalination primarily include composite and asymmetric membranes. Composite membranes consist of multiple layers, typically featuring a thin selective barrier over a porous substrate, enhancing filtration efficiency and durability. In contrast, asymmetric membranes have a non-uniform structure with varying thickness, promoting better flow rates and mechanical strength. Both types are essential in desalination processes to produce potable water from saline sources, with their unique designs suited to different operational requirements and applications in water treatment.</p></p>
