<p><p>The soda feldspar market is primarily divided into two types: plagioclase feldspar and K-feldspar. Plagioclase feldspar, rich in sodium, is commonly used in the glass and ceramics industries due to its ability to lower melting temperatures and enhance thermal stability. K-feldspar, or potassium feldspar, is valued for its high potassium content, making it ideal for ceramics, glass production, and as a flux in various applications. Both types serve essential roles in enhancing product quality and performance across multiple industries.</p></p>
