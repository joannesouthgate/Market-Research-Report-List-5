<p><p>The distribution of electronic components is categorized into two primary types: active and passive components. Active components, such as transistors and integrated circuits, require an external power source to operate and can amplify signals. They play a crucial role in processing and controlling electronic information. In contrast, passive components, like resistors and capacitors, do not require power to function and are used to store or dissipate energy. Together, these components form the backbone of electronic systems, impacting various industries.</p></p>
