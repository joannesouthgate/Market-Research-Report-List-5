<p><p>Electroscopes are devices used to detect electric charge and can be categorized mainly into two types: Pith-Ball and Gold-Leaf. The Pith-Ball electroscope features lightweight pith balls suspended on threads; when charged, they repel or attract, indicating the presence of an electric charge. The Gold-Leaf electroscope uses thin gold leaves that diverge when charged, illustrating the charge's strength. Both types serve educational and experimental purposes, demonstrating the principles of electrostatics in a clear and visual manner.</p></p>
