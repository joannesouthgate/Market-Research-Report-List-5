Machine whole wheat flour is produced using industrial milling processes, offering consistent texture and fine particles, ideal for commercial baking. In contrast, stone whole wheat flour is made by traditional stone milling, preserving more nutrients and flavor, resulting in a coarser texture. Both types cater to different consumer preferences, from health-conscious bakers to those seeking artisanal bread experiences.</p></p>
