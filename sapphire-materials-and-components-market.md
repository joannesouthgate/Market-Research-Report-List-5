<p><p>Sapphire materials and components are categorized based on their formation processes, primarily into Skarn Type and Hydrothermal Type markets. Skarn Type sapphires form through high-temperature metamorphic processes, typically associated with limestone and intrusive igneous rocks, leading to high-quality gemstones. In contrast, Hydrothermal Type sapphires are synthesized in controlled environments that mimic natural conditions, allowing for the production of flawless and large crystals. Both markets cater to different applications, including electronics, optics, and luxury jewelry.</p></p>
