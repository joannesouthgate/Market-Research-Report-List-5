<p><p>Artificial tissue heart valves are medical devices designed to replace damaged or dysfunctional heart valves, typically made from biological materials such as animal tissue. These valves are engineered to mimic the function of natural heart valves, ensuring proper blood flow within the heart. <p><p>The artificial tissue heart valve market is primarily divided into two types: transcatheter heart valves and surgical heart valves. Transcatheter heart valves are minimally invasive devices implanted using catheter-based techniques, offering quicker recovery times and less surgical risk. In contrast, surgical heart valves require open-heart surgery for implantation, providing durable solutions for severe heart valve diseases. Both types cater to varying patient needs, with advancements in materials and technology enhancing their effectiveness and performance in treating valvular heart conditions.</p></p>
